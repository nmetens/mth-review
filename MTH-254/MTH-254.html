Let the points P = (0,3,2), Q = (4,-1,2), R = (1,-1,-3) be on the 3D coordinate plane Now let's find the vector between points P and Q (Note you could also do Q and P, the order does not matter): \[\overrightarrow{PQ}~=~<4-0,-1-3,~2-2>\] \[=~<4,-4,~0>\] And the vector between points P and R (you can also do R and P, Q and R, or R and Q): \[\overrightarrow{PR}~=~<1-0,-1-3,-3-2>\] \[=~<1,-4,-5>\] Now we have two vectors in a plane. Next, we find the normal vector to this plane. We do this by computing the cross product of the vectors \(\overrightarrow{PQ}\) and \(\overrightarrow{PR}\) (remember to follow the matrix co-factor sign pattern): \[\overrightarrow{PQ}~X~\overrightarrow{PR}~=~ \begin{vmatrix} \overrightarrow{i} & \overrightarrow{j} & \overrightarrow{k}\\ 4 & -4 & 0\\ 1 & -4 & -5 \end{vmatrix}~=~\overrightarrow{n}\] \[=~\overrightarrow{i}((-4*-5)-(-4*0))-\overrightarrow{j}((4*-5)-(1*0))+\overrightarrow{k}((4*-4)-(1*-4))\] \[=~\overrightarrow{i}(20-0)-\overrightarrow{j}(-20)-0)+\overrightarrow{k}(-16)--4)\] \[=~\overrightarrow{i}(20)-\overrightarrow{j}(20)+\overrightarrow{k}(-12)\] \[\overrightarrow{n}~=~<20,20,-12>\] Now that we solved for the normal vector, we can pick any of the points on the plane (P, Q, or R). I will pick point P but you can pick any of them and the answer will still be correct. Then we put the point and the normal vector in the vector form line equation (\overrightarrow{r}(t)=+t(a,b,c)): \[\overrightarrow{r}(t)=\overrightarrow{n}+tP\] \[\overrightarrow{r}(t)=<20,20,-12>+t(0,3,2))\] \[\Rightarrow~20(x-0)+20(y-3)-12(z-2)=0\] \[\Rightarrow~20x-0+20y-60-12z+24=0\] \[\Rightarrow~20x+20y-12z=36\] \[\Rightarrow~\frac{5}{4}x+\frac{5}{4}y+\frac{-3}{4}x=\frac{9}{4}\] \[\] And thus, the equation of the plane is:\(~5x+5y-3x=9\)
